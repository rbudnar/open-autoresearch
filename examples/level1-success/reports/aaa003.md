## Likely causal mechanism

Attention pooling preserves position-conditional weighting across the input sequence, recovering signal the mean-pool encoder discarded. The +1.3ms latency cost is consistent with one additional attention computation per forward pass. No alternative explanation observed in the metric pattern.

## Failure modes inspected

- Short-sequence subgroup performance: no qualitative regression observed in per-seed sample inspection (formal subgroup analysis deferred to Level 2+).
- Latency outliers: 99th-percentile latency 24.1ms (vs baseline 22.7ms) — within budget but worth tracking.

## Decision

- Category: `level1_branch_winner` (the highest label available at this level).
- Next action: **graduate to Level 3** before any promotion attempt. Specifically:
  - Add the Skeptic role (§5.7) at Level-2 separation from the Implementation Worker.
  - Run a single-component-swap ablation per §16.1.1: candidate-with-attention-weights-replaced-by-uniform. Confirms the attention pattern (not the surrounding scaffolding) drives the gain.
  - Run candidate at the Level-3 promotion seed count (5 seeds at small tier).
  - Emit a `promotion_request`; let the non-agent verifier sign the packet.
- Reasoning: at Level 1 we have a clean signal but no causal evidence and no independent review. The protocol's §24 promotion ceiling exists specifically to prevent shipping wins at this stage of evidence.
